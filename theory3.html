Complex form

Using Eulers identity, Fourier series can be expressed with complex exponentials. \begin{align*} sin( x + \phi) &= cos(\phi)sin(x) + sin(\phi)cos(x) \\ &= asin(x) + bcos(x) \\ &= a\frac{e^{ix} + e^{-ix}}{2} + b\frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} \\ &= a\frac{e^{ix} + e^{-ix}}{2} - bi\frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2} \\ &= \frac{a - bi}{2}e^{ix} + \frac{a + bi}{2}e^{-ix} \\ &= Ze^{ix} + \overline{Z}e^{-ix} \end{align*} Substituting into the Fourier series \begin{align*} f(x) &= A_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}Z_ne^{ i\frac{2\pi nx}{T_1}} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\overline{Z_n}e^{ -i\frac{2\pi nx}{T_1} } \\ &=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}Z_ne^{ i\frac{2\pi nx}{T_1} } \end{align*}
